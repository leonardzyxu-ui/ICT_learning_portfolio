The "Rake" vs. The "Equation"

Raster Images

I discovered that the word "Raster" comes from the Latin rastrum (rake). This perfectly describes how early screens worked—an electron beam "raking" across the screen row by row.

  • Best for: Photographs, blending colors.
  • Downside: Pixelation when zoomed.
  • Tool: GIMP (Manipulated pixels directly).

Vector Images

Unlike Raster, Vectors are defined by magnitude and direction (Math & Physics). I opened an SVG file in a text editor to prove this.

Growth Mindset

Resilience, negotiation, and the feedback loop.

The "Liquid Glass" Arc

The Challenge: I wanted to create a "Liquid Glass" effect for my website using advanced CSS.

The Failure: I tried everything. Standard CSS filters failed. I tried WebGL, but it broke the site's performance and was too complex for the timeframe. I hit a wall.

The Pivot (Growth): Instead of giving up or submitting broken code, I demonstrated professional communication. I wrote a detailed email to my teacher (Jaydon), explaining the technical blocker, showing my attempts (WebGl), and negotiating a practical change to the requirements.

"I learned that being a developer isn't just about writing code; it's about managing resources and communicating clearly when a solution isn't viable."
