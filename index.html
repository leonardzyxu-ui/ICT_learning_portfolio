The Physics of Pixels (Raster)

Etymology: The "Rake"

I discovered that the word "Raster" is derived from the Latin word rastrum, which means "rake". This is a profound insight into the history of computing. Early CRT monitors functioned by shooting an electron beam that physically "raked" across the screen, line by line, to illuminate phosphors.

Today, we see this legacy in grid-based image formats like JPEG, PNG, and GIF.

Raster Characteristics

  • Best for Photos: Can handle millions of subtle color blends.
  • Resolution Dependent: Zooming in reveals the grid (pixelation).
  • Tool of Choice: GIMP (used for pixel-level manipulation).

The Math of Shapes (Vector)

Unlike Raster images, Vectors are not grids. They are mathematical equations describing Magnitude and Direction. This makes them infinitely scalable.

How Computers "See" Images

My "Question of the Day" research revealed that at the deepest level, images are just strings of 1s and 0s.

In a black-and-white bitmap, '0' might represent white and '1' might represent black. When these bits are arranged in a grid with a specific width (metadata), an image emerges from the numbers.

"I learned that what looks like a picture to us is just a massive spreadsheet of numbers to the computer."

Phase 1: Empathy

The Client Interview

I interviewed Mary, a partner who needed a website to support her piano learning hobby. Through our interview, I identified her core frustration: existing sites were too cluttered.

The Goal: She needed a "Zen" space—simple, friendly, and focused purely on "Practice Tips" and "Easy Songs" for beginners.

"I realized that the Design Cycle isn't about what *I* want to build, but about solving the specific pain points of the user."
Phase 4: Prototype & Fix

The "Absolute" Disaster

The Problem

In my early prototype for the Piano site, I used Absolute Positioning for the black keys. It looked perfect on my screen.

⚠️ Failure: As soon as I resized the window or checked on mobile, the keys floated away from the keyboard. The layout shattered.

The Solution

I used AI to help refactor the CSS logic. I switched from absolute coordinates to Relative Positioning within a flex container.

✅ Success: The keys became "children" of the white keys, moving perfectly in sync regardless of screen size.

AI & Code Strategy

Using Artificial Intelligence as a co-pilot, not a crutch. Plus, the story of my biggest technical failure.

The "Liquid Glass" Resilience Arc

The Ambition: I wanted a complex "Liquid Glass" distortion effect for my website background. I was obsessed with making it look premium.

The Wall: I spent hours trying standard CSS filters. They looked cheap. I tried implementing WebGL. It broke the site's performance, dropping the frame rate to unplayable levels. I was stuck.

"Instead of ghosting or submitting broken code, I demonstrated professional communication."

The Pivot: I wrote a detailed email to my teacher (Jaydon). I explained the technical blocker, attached my failed WebGL code as evidence of effort, and proposed a cleaner, static glassmorphism alternative. He accepted immediately.

Prompt Engineering: Vague vs. Specific

During the Falcon 9 project, I learned that AI output is only as good as the input. I documented my prompt iterations to prove this.

The Engineer Prompt

Result: Production-ready code that I could use immediately.
